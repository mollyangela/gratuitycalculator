Gratuity Calculation Overview

Gratuity is calculated based on the employee's last basic salary and the number of years worked. The calculation method differs for limited and unlimited contracts.

Gratuity for Limited Contracts

For employees on limited contracts, the gratuity is calculated as follows:

  • Less than 5 years of service: Employees receive 21 days of salary for each year of service.
  • 5 or more years of service: Employees receive 30 days of salary for each year of service after the completion of five years.

Gratuity for Unlimited Contracts

For employees on unlimited contracts, the calculation is similar:

  • Less than 5 years of service: Employees receive 21 days of salary for each year of service.
  • 5 or more years of service: Employees receive 30 days of salary for each year of service after the completion of five years.

Typical Gratuity Percentages

The typical gratuity percentages can be summarized as follows:

  • For the first 5 years of service: 21 days of salary per year, which translates to approximately 70% of a month's salary for each year worked.
  • For each additional year beyond 5 years: 30 days of salary per year, which is equivalent to 100% of a month's salary for each year worked.
